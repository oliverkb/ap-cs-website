Snap! Drawing App

Oliver's Drawing App

This is my first major programming project from AP CSP. My partner Josh and I programmed a draggable pen tool, polygon tool, star tool, daisy tool, circle tool, and eraser tool, with a directions popup displaying keybinds. Color is manipulable through an RGB slider, and line thickness through a slide as well.

Mexican National Football League The Game

Scratch Game

This is a scratch football game I made with Riley, Noah, and Lucas. I mainly worked on code. Riley worked on code, glitch finding, and backstory. Noah worked on refining graphics, and Lucas on sound design and some early character design. This was a project that we were given around a month to complete, and it took us as long. The biggest challenge for me (and most frustrating part) was fixing glitches Riley found, especially with all the moving parts to the game, and the most fun was implementing new mechanics like power and aiming. According to Riley, our game includes 1200+ blocks of code.

Google Doc

For my spring Genius Hour, I used Tinkercad (a 3-D modeling program), to design a Beechcraft King Air F90, and two flight paths, which I planned to 3D print. This project was in conjunction with a Calculus BC project on efficiency of different flight paths.
